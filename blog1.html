Diet Plan

Fad and low-fat diets

Low-fat diets – they are adaptable, popular with both dietitians and cooks at home. They’re fun and stylistic diets that create more of an idea about the way to eat, and it’s more of a method of thinking when designing dishes than a regimented meal plan. But I never said those big-name diets were terrible! Remember all the food fads that I listed? Those had their moments in the sun, and faded out of popularity, whether it was due to lack of scientific backing, reputation or that they were just plain gross, they’re gone! Today let’s look at two of my favorite, tried and tested, “battle-plan diets.”

1. Atkins diet

Okay, last time we took a look at that fun and challenging Paleo diet. One of the big themes is removing carbs. Another diet, much more famous and recognizable, with a steady following, is the Atkins diet. It works on the same principle. Removing those (delicious) but tricky carbs. Where the Paleo focuses more on general health, the Atkins is a “battle-plan diet.” It’s objective being weight loss. Carb heavy diets, which let’s be honest are a lot of American staples, can be very damaging to one’s body. Low-fat diets are not much better either. Each body processes sugar differently, and diet has a huge say in how that metabolism works.

2. Weight Watchers

Here is another “battle-plan diet” that people have been using a long time. It’s practical, accessible, and straightforward. It works on an entirely different system than Atkins, and it’s one I think is fun. They basically run off of this point system that allows you to track intake, food group use, and spend on new recipes to try out. have a revolutionary use of social media. use a community system to keep you accountable to other users. Now let’s get down to the food. Weight Watchers may focus more on weight than say the Mediterranean, but don’t worry! Their system is very health-nut friendly. The diet is based on healthy fats, low-fat adaptations, cooking out or eliminating the bad ones. Lean proteins, and replacing processed with natural sugars (i.e., fruits and veggies!). Here is when you monitor yourself, re-introduce healthier carbs, and continue to use plans to keep weight off!

3. Dolce Diet

Now here is a mix of passions for me. The Dolce diet is probably a little more obscure for a lot of you. Mike Dolce is a genius in the Mixed Martial Arts community. He’s been behind champions and contenders alike. His diet is revolutionary. It’s meant for both the high-level athlete, and the everyday working Joe. The plan works with a very client-interfaced approach. You control everything, the menu plan, the fitness aspect, and the results themselves. The food side of the diet is tailored to you and your goals, high in lean protein, pre-workout meals, post-workout meals, eating every two hours to boost metabolism. It’s very regimented, for you who like that, it’s also very lean. It isn’t the colorful foodie diet like the Mediterranean or new trendy challenge like Paleo. But it’s what you’d expect from MMA. It will help you stay lean in a friendly and easy way to use. With motivation and results streamed to you live! It has a distinct focus on personal development. Step one, you have the visible weight loss and those feel-good feelings. Step two you have a major increase in energy and metabolism. Stage three is where those lasting habits and weight loss comes in. You adjust to having a low fat and sugar way of life and become quite comfortable living this way. That’s a look at two of the more regimented diets. Personally, I prefer more the stylistic diets. Ones that I can adapt and use in a personal way. I find them to be more colorful, more flavorful, and yet still practical. But if your goal is to shred down those pounds using a helpful system, strategy, and community, then this style may be more what you’re looking for.
