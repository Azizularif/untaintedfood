7-Day Healthy Blood Pressure Meal Plan


Keep Fit

Numbers don’t lie, and the hard truth is that more than 100 million adults in the US suffer from high blood pressure, also known as hypertension, according to the American Heart Association. On top of that, it is thought that the number of people who are at risk for this condition keeps rising by the day. As of 2013, up to 103,000 people died of high blood pressure. Currently, an estimated 103 million adults are suffering from this scourge, which is a major concern. What exactly is Blood Pressure? Blood pressure, also known as hypertension is the pressure exerted by blood on artery walls. This pressure is measured in mmHg, meaning millimeters of mercury. Upon checkup, if your readings indicate 120/80mmHg, your clinician would often refer to it as 120 over 80. The primary cause of this deadly condition is often not clear; however, your family history, eating pattern especially salty foods, drinking, weight, and lack of physical activities are known to cause it. Some medicinal drugs can also raise blood pressure. How to Control Blood Pressure It is a condition just like any other disease. However, no matter how deadly it is, it can be adequately controlled. Losing extra pounds, regular exercise, limiting alcohol intake, quit smoking, cutting back on caffeine, reducing your stress, monitoring your blood pressure from time to time, and having a healthy diet is vital for hypertension patients. Again certain foods are recommended for patients suffering from hypertension. These foods will regulate your blood flow as well as ease the pressure. Here is an example of the seven days meal plan for people suffering from this manageable condition.

Hypertension Patient’s Meal Plan for Seven Days

Monday

• A bowl of oats with milk and 1 banana would do for breakfast • For lunch, take summer salad with balsamic vinaigrette • Apple Pecan Chicken for dinner • A banana

Tuesday

• Start your day with chocolate peanut butter with a banana smoothie • Lunch; opt for canned tuna in oil or even water and salad • Roasted salmon and asparagus for dinner • Potato crisp

Wednesday

• Grapefruit Green Smoothie • Quinoa salad with nuts would be perfect for lunch • Dinner would be well chipotle chicken with sweet potato skins • Snacking is as well important, consider taking 5-7 ounces plain Greek yogurt and a small banana

Thursday

• Apple walnut quinoa for breakfast • Consider Pumpkin Soup • Green lentil & walnut Bolognese works every time for dinner • A banana

Friday

• Consider having chocolate peanut butter smoothie for breakfast • Roasted sweet potato salad would be perfect for lunch • Choose and eat your morning or lunch leftovers for dinner on Friday night • A banana for a snack

Saturday

• Apple walnut quinoa for breakfast • Fresh spring rolls • Grilled honey lime chicken kebabs • For snacks, consider microwave popcorn

Sunday

• For breakfast, consider Coconut milk quinoa pudding • Consider eating any leftovers you may have • Bibimbap nourishing bowl • Sweet potato chips would make perfect snacks

Conclusion

Following this dietary plan should assist you in managing your condition. There are also many meal plans around; however, researching for the best one needs quality time. It is, nonetheless, of import to know that hypertension is not a death sentence. It is a condition that can be managed with a plan diet plan and the right medication.
